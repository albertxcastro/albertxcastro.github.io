Multiple linear regression

Multiple linear regression is a statistical technique used to model the relationship between two or more independent variables and a single dependent variable, by fitting a linear equation to observed data. It extends the concept of simple linear regression, which models the relationship between two variables, to include multiple predictors, allowing for the analysis of more complex relationships among variables (Hair, Black, Babin, & Anderson, 2014). y = b0 + b1x1 + b2x2 + ... + bnxn + ε
Where:

  • y is the predicted value of the dependent variable.
  • b0 is the intercept (also known as the constant or the y-intercept)
  • b1, b2, ..., bn are the regression coefficients for the respective independent variables x1, x2, ..., xn.
  • x1, x2, ..., xn are the independent variables.
  • ε is the error term that represents the random variation in the dependent variable that is not explained by the independent variables (Hair et al., 2014).
