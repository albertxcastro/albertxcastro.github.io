Reflective activity - Inappropriate Use of Surveys

Reading the article written by Cadwalladr, C., & Graham-Harrison, E. (2018), I can see how harmful the Cambridge Analytica case is, as it violated several user privacy rights and was misused for political reasons.

It is very surprising to me that during the whole master’s course, all units put a lot of emphasis on how important ethics and professionalism are, and while reading this case (the Cambridge Analytica case), I see why it is so important to be reiterative on this.

Moreover, when reading more and more about Cambridge Analytica, I can only find more and more violations of the code of Ethics and professional conduct:

  1. The manipulation of information for political purposes is a clear example of unethical use of data.
  2. Lack of informed consent to users who participated in the quiz. This harms Consent and Transparency.
  3. When the scandal exploded, it showed how personal data was protected (or unprotected) on social media platforms like Facebook. Additionally, It highlighted how malicious organizations can exploit the data to accomplish their own interests, good or bad, for the public interest.
  4. It demonstrated the lack of regulations on data usage. This brought the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) to the European Union in 2018 (Wolford).

In conclusion, the Cambridge Analytica scandal emphasizes the need for stringent ethical standards, more robust laws on data protection and privacy, and a collective effort between organizations and individuals to ensure data's transparent and responsible usage.
